> Analysing Blackpool's employment landscape and identifying strategic opportunities for Fedcap Employment.

## Overview

Blackpool has one of the highest unemployment rates in the UK at **8.6%** nearly double the national average of 4.3%. Combined with an economic inactivity rate of **31.6%** and a youth unemployment rate of **14.2%**, the data reveals deep structural challenges in the local labour market.

This report uses ONS employment data to diagnose those challenges and surface four concrete opportunities for Fedcap Employment to create meaningful impact in the region.

## Key Findings

### 1. Unemployment & Benefits Dependency

Blackpool's unemployment rate is nearly **double the national average**, with a claimant rate of 6.1% against England's 3.8%.

| Metric             | Blackpool | Benchmark        | Gap     |
|:-------------------|:---------:|:----------------:|--------:|
| Unemployment Rate  | 8.6%      | 4.3% (National)  | +4.3pp  |
| Claimant Rate      | 6.1%      | 3.8% (England)   | +2.3pp  |

### 2. Economic Inactivity

At 31.6%, Blackpool's economic inactivity rate far exceeds both the North West (23.7%) and England (21.3%) averages **48% above the England average**.

| Reason                          | Share |
|:--------------------------------|------:|
| Retirement                      | 53%   |
| Long-term sickness / disability | 19%   |
| Looking after home or family    | 11%   |
| Other                           | 9%    |
| Student                         | 8%    |

### 3. Youth Employment

Young people face compounding disadvantages with low qualification levels and high rates of economic disengagement.

| Indicator                    | Rate  |
|:-----------------------------|------:|
| Youth Unemployment (16–24)   | 14.2% |
| NEET Rate                    | 12.8% |
| In Full-time Education       | 38.4% |

> Blackpool has a significantly lower share of NVQ4+ holders and a higher share of residents with no qualifications compared to the national average.
